Summary 
What goes in the summary? This is the most important design choice. A bad summary loses critical information; a good one captures it concisely. The summary should preserve:

The user's original goal
Key facts the agent has established
What tools it has tried and what worked/didn't
Any decisions made or constraints discovered

Compression
The flow in one paragraph

Before every LLM call, we count tokens. If under 8000, do nothing. If over 8000, find the cutoff between "old turns to compress" and "recent turns to keep" — the cutoff is always on an assistant-message boundary so tool_call_ids stay linked. Slice the messages list into three pieces: (1) keep the system prompt and original user goal, (2) replace the older middle chunk with a single LLM-generated summary, (3) keep the last 4 turn groups intact. Concatenate. Use this compressed list for the next LLM call.

Embeddings, explained without math
An embedding is a way of turning a piece of text into a list of numbers — typically a few hundred or a few thousand numbers — such that texts with similar meanings produce similar lists of numbers

This is genuinely strange and magical when you first encounter it. How do you turn the meaning of "Caligula" into a list of numbers? You don't write rules. You train a neural network on huge amounts of text — like an LLM, but with a different objective. The network learns to produce lists of numbers such that semantically-similar things end up with similar lists. It works because language has structure that can be captured in geometric relationships between vectors.
